Welcome to *Teaching for Impact: Designing Effective & Open Training*. This workbook is your practical guide to designing and delivering training that actually works — grounded in evidence-based teaching practices and the principles of open science.

Whether you're planning your first workshop or refining an approach you've used for years, you'll find concrete strategies here that you can put to use straight away.

### Who developed this workbook

Two South African organisations developed this workbook. Both specialise in building capacity for research, open science, and professional skills development:

**[Talarify](https://www.talarify.co.za/)** is a consulting and training organisation focused on capacity and community development in open science and digital and computational research.

**[Prelude](https://prelude.tech)** is a consulting and training organisation focused on professional skills development in Python, teamwork, and education. Prelude also builds edTech products.

### The FeME Data for Change Training Fund

We created this workbook for grantees of the [Failure Modes of Engineering (FeME)](https://feme.ac.uk/) Seed Funding instrument — specifically the Data for Change Training Fund (2026).

FeME tackles engineering solutions to climate change and biodiversity loss, with particular attention to their impact on women, children, and underrepresented communities. The programme is a collaboration between the University of Edinburgh, the University of Glasgow, and Heriot-Watt University, funded by the UK Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council (EPSRC) through the Tomorrow's Engineering Research Challenges Network Plus Programme (2025–2028).

### How to use this workbook

You can use this workbook in two ways:

- **As self-study material.** Work through it at your own pace. If you take this route, we strongly recommend exploring the further reading and reference materials — they will deepen your understanding considerably.
- **As a companion to the interactive workshops** run by facilitators with experience in evidence-based teaching and open science. The workbook reinforces and extends what you cover in those sessions.
