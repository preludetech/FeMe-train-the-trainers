# Research: Learning Styles Myth and Multimodal Learning

## Claims verified

1. **"Learning styles is pseudoscience"** — CORRECT (scientific consensus). Pashler et al. (2008) conducted a comprehensive review and found no evidence that matching instruction to self-reported learning styles improves outcomes. Over 70 different learning style instruments exist, but none have been validated by the kind of evidence needed (crossover interaction studies).

2. **"Preference doesn't predict results"** — CORRECT. People have preferences for certain media, but no study has shown that teaching to an identified learning style results in better retention or learning outcomes.

3. **"Mixing media IS useful"** — CORRECT, but for different reasons than learning styles theory. Supported by:
   - **Dual Coding Theory** (Paivio, 1986): Humans process verbal and visual information through separate but interconnected systems. Using both enhances memory.
   - **Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning** (Mayer, 2009): "People learn more deeply from words and pictures than from words alone." Multiple representations reduce overload and enhance transfer to long-term memory.

4. **"Clinging to a preferred style can harm progress"** — CORRECT framing. If learners avoid effective strategies (like practice) in favour of comfortable ones (like watching videos), they miss opportunities for deeper learning.
