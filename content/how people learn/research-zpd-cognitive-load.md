# Research: ZPD vs Cognitive Load Theory

## Issue identified: the "Cognitive load" section conflates three different concepts

The section headed "Cognitive load" (line 49 of `1-clean.md`) describes ideas from three distinct theories:

### 1. Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky, 1978)

The "too hard = discouraged, too easy = bored, sweet spot needed" framing maps to Vygotsky's ZPD — the gap between what a learner can do alone and what they can achieve with guidance. The ZPD is about finding the right level of challenge. This is what the section is primarily describing.

- Source: https://www.simplypsychology.org/zone-of-proximal-development.html
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zone_of_proximal_development

### 2. Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller, 1988)

Cognitive Load Theory (CLT) is a *different* concept. CLT is about working memory limitations — how much information a learner's brain can process simultaneously. It focuses on instructional design that reduces unnecessary mental effort (extraneous load) while optimising productive mental effort (germane load). CLT does share the "too much = overwhelmed" idea, but it's specifically about working memory capacity, not about finding the right difficulty level.

- Source: https://www.brainscape.com/academy/cognitive-load-theory-definition/

### 3. Growth Mindset (Dweck, 2006)

The statement "growth comes from effort" and the idea that learners avoid hard things because they think "I'm not good at this" maps to Carol Dweck's growth mindset research, not to ZPD or CLT. Dweck distinguishes between fixed mindset (ability is innate) and growth mindset (ability develops through effort). Note: Dweck herself cautioned (2015) that effort alone is insufficient — strategies and seeking help also matter.

## Specific problematic claims

1. **"Learners often believe their zone of proximal development is lower than it really is"** — This is NOT a standard ZPD claim. ZPD is assessed externally by an instructor, not by learner self-perception. This claim better maps to self-efficacy theory (Bandura, 1997).

2. **"Growth comes from effort"** — This is growth mindset (Dweck), not ZPD. Vygotsky's ZPD emphasises social support and scaffolding.

## Recommendation

The section should either:
- Be restructured to properly separate these concepts (ZPD, cognitive load, growth mindset)
- Or pick the concept that best serves the workbook's purpose and present it accurately

The core message (courses should aim for the right difficulty level) is sound — it's the labelling and attribution that needs fixing.
