# Research: Motivation in Adult Learning and Training

## 1. Key Motivation Theories Relevant to Adult Learning

### 1.1 Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)

Self-Determination Theory (SDT) identifies three innate psychological needs that are essential for motivation, well-being, and optimal functioning:

- **Autonomy**: The need to feel ownership and initiative in one's actions. Supported by experiences of interest and value; undermined by external control.
- **Competence**: The need to feel effective and capable of mastering challenges. A sense that one can succeed and grow.
- **Relatedness**: The need for connection with others and a sense of belonging.

When these three needs are satisfied, people show enhanced self-motivation and mental health. When thwarted, motivation and well-being diminish. Both intrinsic motivation and well-internalised forms of extrinsic motivation predict positive outcomes across varied educational levels and cultural contexts, and are enhanced by supports for these three basic needs.

**Sources**:
- Ryan, R.M. & Deci, E.L. (2000). Self-determination theory and the facilitation of intrinsic motivation, social development, and well-being. *American Psychologist*, 55(1), 68-78. [PDF](https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/SDT/documents/2000_RyanDeci_SDT.pdf) | [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11392867/)
- Ryan, R.M. & Deci, E.L. (2020). Intrinsic and extrinsic motivation from a self-determination theory perspective. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0361476X20300254)

### 1.2 Intrinsic vs Extrinsic Motivation

- **Intrinsic motivation**: Driven by personal satisfaction, curiosity, and the inherent enjoyment of learning.
- **Extrinsic motivation**: Performing a task to earn a reward or avoid punishment.

Key findings: Intrinsic motivation is "by far" the strongest predictor of positive outcomes including engagement, job satisfaction, and commitment (meta-analysis of 124 workplace studies). Intrinsic motivation had 3x greater impact on employee engagement than extrinsic motivators.

**Sources**:
- [How Motivation Affects Adult Learning - Advance the Seed](https://www.advancetheseed.org/blog/how-motivation-affects-adult-learning-intrinsic-vs-extrinsic-drivers)
- [Using intrinsic and extrinsic motivation in continuing professional education - PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4825346/)

### 1.3 Expectancy-Value Theory (Eccles & Wigfield)

Core formula: **Expectancy x Value = Motivation**

- **Expectancy**: "Can I do this?" — the learner's belief about whether they can succeed.
- **Value**: "Why should I do this?" — broken into intrinsic value, attainment value, utility value, and cost.

If either expectancy OR value is zero, motivation collapses (multiplicative relationship).

**Sources**:
- Wigfield, A. & Eccles, J.S. (2000). Expectancy-Value Theory of Achievement Motivation. [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0361476X99910159) | [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10620382/)

### 1.4 Andragogy (Knowles) — Adult Learning Theory

Six principles: need to know, self-concept, experience, readiness to learn, orientation to learning, motivation. Adults are primarily motivated by internal factors rather than external ones.

**Sources**:
- [Adult Learning Theory: Andragogy of Malcolm Knowles - eLearning Industry](https://elearningindustry.com/the-adult-learning-theory-andragogy-of-malcolm-knowles)
- [Andragogy in Practice: Applying to Team Science Training - PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11008574/)

## 2. Claim: "Adults choose to be in your training... you can't rely on external structures"

### Verdict: Mostly accurate, with important nuances

The research supports that adults are internally motivated and self-directing, and generally lack external structures present in formal education. However, not all adult participation is voluntary — employers often send employees to mandatory training. Even in mandatory contexts, the core insight holds: you must actively create conditions for engagement.

## 3. Task Difficulty and Motivation

### Verdict: Well-supported by multiple frameworks

**Flow Theory (Csikszentmihalyi)**: Flow occurs when challenge matches skill level. Too challenging = anxiety. Too easy = boredom. Just right = flow state.

**Sources**:
- [Flow (psychology) - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flow_(psychology))
- [Flow Theory - Newcastle University TheoryHub](https://open.ncl.ac.uk/academic-theories/8/flow-theory/)

**Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky)**: The productive zone between what a learner can do independently and what they cannot do even with support.

**Sources**:
- [Zone of Proximal Development - Simply Psychology](https://www.simplypsychology.org/zone-of-proximal-development.html)

## 4. Practical Strategies for Maintaining Motivation

- Make relevance explicit ("what's in it for me")
- Support autonomy through choices
- Calibrate challenge (Goldilocks zone / ZPD)
- Provide clear goals and frequent feedback
- Build connection and community
- Vary activities (change every ~20 minutes)
- Build confidence (address both expectancy and value)

**Sources**:
- [CDC: How to Captivate and Motivate Adult Learners (PDF)](https://www.cdc.gov/training-development/media/pdfs/adult-learning-guide-508.pdf)
- [10 High Impact Teaching Strategies for Adult Learners - Training Industry](https://trainingindustry.com/articles/strategy-alignment-and-planning/10-high-impact-teaching-strategies-for-adult-learners/)

## 5. How Motivation Connects to Mindsets

Growth mindset supports the expectancy component ("I believe I can improve") and the competence need in SDT. Stress-is-enhancing mindset helps learners persist through productive struggle rather than avoiding challenge.

**Summary table**:

| Theory | Core Question | Key Insight for Trainers |
|---|---|---|
| Self-Determination Theory | What psychological needs drive motivation? | Support autonomy, competence, and relatedness |
| Expectancy-Value Theory | Why do learners engage or disengage? | Must believe they can succeed AND that it's worth it |
| Andragogy (Knowles) | How do adults learn differently? | Need relevance, self-direction, respect for experience |
| Flow Theory | When are learners most engaged? | When challenge matches skill level |
| Growth Mindset (Dweck) | How do beliefs about ability affect effort? | Believing you can grow → embracing challenge |
| Stress Mindset (Crum) | How do beliefs about stress affect learning? | Viewing stress as enhancing → better performance |
