Setting learning objectives that can lead to the expected outcomes before getting started on content/deciding on activities (Backward Design framework — start with learning goals, then assessments, then activities) [blooms taxonomy]

Dont just start creating content, you need to have an end goal in mind. 
Make sure things are modular, and that the modules are coherent 
How to think about tying assessments back to objectives, what should people know or be able to do at the end of your training 
How can activities be used to meet objectives 
This helps to prevent scope creep, it's always tempting to add too much into a course
