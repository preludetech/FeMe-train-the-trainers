# Research: Bloom's Taxonomy

## Claims verified

1. **Bloom's as a framework for knowledge vs understanding** — The characterization in the text (connected knowledge, applying knowledge, analysing, creativity) is a FAIR simplification of Bloom's levels. The original taxonomy (Bloom, 1956) had six levels: Knowledge, Comprehension, Application, Analysis, Synthesis, Evaluation. The revised taxonomy (Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001) reordered to: Remember, Understand, Apply, Analyse, Evaluate, Create.

2. **Anti-pattern: progressing bottom-to-top** — WELL-SUPPORTED. Research has challenged the assumption of strict linear progression:
   - Empirical evidence shows direct connections between nonconsecutive categories (e.g., comprehension to analysis)
   - Mastery of simpler categories may not be required for mastery of more complex ones
   - The revised taxonomy acknowledges a "relaxed hierarchy" rather than a strict one
   - Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9727608/

3. **"Understanding helps memory because seeking understanding leads to memorization as a side effect"** — CORRECT. This is consistent with levels-of-processing theory (Craik & Lockhart, 1972): deeper processing (understanding meaning, making connections) produces stronger memory traces than shallow processing (rote repetition).
